CSS: Display Inline vs. Inline Block

5/14/15

While learning CSS I was completely befuddled by the display property. I tried to learn by doing. I fiddled around with HTML elements in CSS. Most concepts came naturally to me. That was, until I bumped into the display property. W3 Schools tells us that, "The display property specifies the type of box used for an HTML element." Simple enough right? Not for me. I tried to mess around with it and either my div wouldn't move or it'd move in unpredictable ways.

A quick search on Stack Overflow returned some really easy to digest information on the subject:

Inline Elements:

1) Respect left & right margins and padding, but not top & bottom
2) Cannot have a width and height set
3) Allow other elements to sit to their left and right.

Block elements:

1) Respect left & right margins and padding, but not top & bottom
2) Force a line break after the block element

Inline-block elements:

1) Allow other elements to sit to their left and right
2) Respect top & bottom margins and padding
3) Respect height and width
